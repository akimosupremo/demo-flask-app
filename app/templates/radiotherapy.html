About Radiotherapy

Radiotherapy, also known as radiation therapy, is a treatment modality that uses ionizing radiation to kill cancer cells and shrink tumors. It works by damaging the DNA inside the cancer cells, making it difficult for them to grow and divide. Radiotherapy is used to treat a variety of cancers, including breast, lung, and prostate cancers, among others.

Radiotherapy is delivered externally, using a machine called a linear accelerator, or internally, through the placement of radioactive sources in or near the tumor. The treatment is typically given 5 days a week for several weeks and is usually combined with other cancer treatments such as surgery or chemotherapy.

Radiotherapy is a safe and effective treatment option for many patients with cancer, but it can cause side effects, such as fatigue, skin irritation, and nausea. Your healthcare team will work with you to manage these side effects and ensure that you receive the best possible care during and after your treatment.
